Dictionary Entry Example

liber, -bri m.

Meanings: book, volume; the inner bark of a tree.

  • libri — books, works of literature
  • in libris — “in books,” used figuratively for learning
Inter arma silent libri. — Proverb

verbum, -i n.

Meanings: word, expression, saying.

  • verba facere — to make speeches
  • verbum dare — to deceive, “give a word”
Verba volant, scripta manent. — Latin proverb (“Spoken words fly, written words remain”)
